About Me

Hi, this is Jung-Chun Liu 劉容均 (/li:'oʊ rɔŋ 'dʒyʊn/), or you can just call me Joan. I am a first-year CSE Ph.D. student at the University of Michigan advised by Joyce Chai. I visited Computational Cognitive Science Lab working with Jiayuan Mao in 2024.

Previously, I was a research assistant working with Professor Shao-Hua Sun at NTU Robot Learning Lab. I received my master degree from National Taiwan University, where I was advised by Professor Tian-Li Yu at Taiwan Evolutionary Intelligence Laboratory. I received my B.S. degree with double majors in Electrical Engineering and Psychology from National Taiwan University in 2021.

My curiosity centers on symbol emergence, which starts from a fundamental question: why do people pursue symbolism? Although neural scaling appears to offer a route to intelligent behavior in neural science and machine learning, why does symbolism remain essential? I propose the ultimate intelligence comprises a group of individual neural systems and a shared symbol system. In individual intelligence, cognitive activities is unified and scalable, yet representation and processes are differentiated for diverse capabilities. Meanwhile, the shared symbol system emerge, giving rise to collective intelligence that transcend any single intelligence.
