Research

Behold the icons that visualize what you’re all about. or just take up space. your call bro.

My research focuses on the integration of human cognition and perception across various domains to address real-world comprehensive problems. Specifically, I investigate the decomposition of cognitive modules and their interactive dynamics. During my undergraduate studies, I conducted a psychological research project exploring the disparities in eye movements on cognitive and perceptual workload. The result shows opposite effects of the two types of mental workload on fixation-related parameters. This study revealed insights into the potential separation of cognitive and perceptual processes, evident in behavioral patterns. For my master's research, I developed a framework that automatically induces subtasks and constructs task structures to tackle hierarchical and compositional decision-making problems. The framework decompose high-level abstractions and low-level execution for classical planning and deep reinforcement learning respectively. This study demonstrates the integration of inductive and deductive learning. Additionally, I employed genetic programming to induce symbolic rules, which model selection plays the crucial rule on whether the rules are learnable. By configuring appropriate models based on prior knowledge, we decompose the domain knowledge from the task level to the skill level.
